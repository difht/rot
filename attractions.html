Hot Tourist Destinations Everyone Loves!

Gale Crater

The Gale Crater is 96 miles in diameter and estimated to be around 3.5-3.8 billion years old. The crater itself was named after Walter Frederick Gale, an amateur astronomer from Sydeny, Austrailia, who observed Mars from the 18th century. Mount Sharp is a mountain at the center of Gale and it rises 18,000 feet high. The Gale Crater is a hot tourist destination as it strikes curiousity in many onlookers due to this specific location potentially having the thickest exposed sections of layered sedimentary rocks in the solar system.

Olympus Mons

The largest of the volcanoes in the Tharsis Montes region stands Olympus Mons. An extensive shield volcanoe 624km in diameter and approximately the same size as the state of Arizona. With an astonishing height of 25km, rimmed by a 6km high scarp. With a caldera located at the summit of Olympus Mons. Olympus Mons is a great place and opportunity for great explorations and exciting treks.

Valles Mariners

Valles Mariners, or Valles Valley, is a vast canyon arrangement that runs along the Martian equator just East of the Tharsis region. Valles Mariners is 4000km (2500 miles) long and reaches depths up to 4 miles! for comparison, the Grand Canyon on Earth is only 500 miles longand 1 mile deep. In fact, the extent of the height of Valles Mariners is as long as the United States!
